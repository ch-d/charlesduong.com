PRINCIPLES

Each problem is different, but I believe a certain cadence can be adopted to build intuitive experiences. In order to navigate the complex network of problem sets, I established three principles that keep me grounded when working on design.

Thoughtful Process

Determining who the user is and an overarching goal at the beginning is invaluable to me. Setting these guidelines early on constantly reminds me that my designs are not about me or my successes; the user and their needs are the main priority. It also allows me to navigate the ambiguous nature of design with confidence. The pixels will come, but only after understanding the metrics of success and trusting the process to get there.

Do Not Repeat Yourself

I believe that modular design systems can create an agile environment where wireframes and ideas are pushed out efficiently. Reducing redundant tasks allow more time to prototype concepts and assess incorrect assumptions. Foundational design principles may be important, but testing past intuitions in an evolving market is more valuable to me. By automating repetitive tasks, I can focus more time on use cases that matter and iterate from there.

Holistic Storytelling

What is the story the feature is trying to encourage? What is the hero of the design? What are the feelings presented when using the object and its functions? Through asking questions, I am given the context I need to understand the whole story and the constraints of a problem. By seeking qualitative and quantitative data, I feel more at ease breaking it down to its core essence. Although there are endless considerations when presenting designs, I believe it is best communicated as a concise and meaningful story.
